Lesson learned from work

A typical scrum process

  1. daily stand ups
  2. biweekly sprint planning (1 hr) to assign tasks to make sure everyone has capacity fulfilled. For biweekly, spare one day for meetings (varies)
  3. biweekly backlog grooming (1 hr), alternates with sprint planning. The team get together to look at backlog.
    1. make sure new added tasks all have estimates (story point and time estimation)
    2. make sure high priority items at top
    3. make sure top items are enough for team to work on for 2 sprints (4 weeks for biweekly sprints)
  4. monthly retrospective meetings
    1. kudos
    2. what went well
    3. what needs improvement
    4. action items

Task estimation

When estimating time required to finish a task. Make estimation based on engineering effort, then apply 1.4 factor to account for code reviews and discussions etc.

Design review process

  1. send out document before meeting so that others can take a look before time and leave comments
  2. at review meeting, spend first 10-15 mins (depending on length) for others to read the doc and leave comments.
  3. while waiting, address small comments while they come in by responding in the doc
  4. after everyone finish reading, start conversation by asking general questions or feedbacks before diving into comments
  5. This usually leads to more in depth conversations
  6. After all conversations are addressed, dive into documented comments in the doc and go over them all
  7. Answer additional comments if they are not addressed.

Tip: before the meeting, delegate a person to take note for you. Spare a section at end of document for note taking, with date, attendees, location etc.
